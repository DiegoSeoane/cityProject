Toponymy


"Pyongyang" literally means "flat land", one of its historical names is Ryugyŏng (in Chosŏn'gŭl, 류경; revised romanization of Korean, ryugyeong), or "willow capital", since throughout history, the The city has always had a large number of willow trees, and many poems deal with them. Today, willows are still abundant in the city. Many buildings and places have "Ryugyŏng" in their name, such as the unfinished Ryugyong Hotel. Other historical names include Kisŏng, Hwangsŏng, Rangrang, Sŏgyŏng, Sŏdo, Hogyŏng, Changan, etc. During the Japanese occupation, the city was known as Heijō (平壌?), which is the Japanese reading of Chinese characters (in Hanja, 平壌).
